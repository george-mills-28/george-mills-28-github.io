What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?


Imagine you have a picture frame:

  • Padding - This is like the space between the picture and the inside edge of the frame. It’s the area where you might put a mat or a soft backing to keep the picture from touching the frame directly. In web design, padding is the space inside the border of an element, between the content and the border.
  • Border - This is the actual frame itself. It surrounds the picture and separates it from the outside world. In web design, the border is a line or an outline that goes around the padding and content of an element.
  • Margin - This is the space between the outer edge of the frame and the next object or wall. It’s like the empty space around the frame where you can’t put anything else. In web design, the margin is the space outside the border, separating one element from another.

    • To summerise:

  • Padding - Space inside the border, around the content
  • Border - The line or edge surrounding the padding.
  • Margin - Space outside the border, separating the element from other elements.
